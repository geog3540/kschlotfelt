Percent Hispanic in Minnesota Counties

This was the first map we made on Observable and the most coding I had done in over 4 years. As far as patterns go, we can see that there isn't a huge hispanic population across Minnesota. I had expected that the two counties with St. Paul and Minneapolis (Ramsey and Hennepin) to have a higher percent of hispanic population, only becuase those have, in general, a higher total population. Yet, they're in the middle between 3% and 15%. In the last assignment when I saw that there were two counties that really stand out, I assumed that they would be the Twin Cities. We do see that there tends to be a higher percentage of hispanic further south - there appears to be fewer in the most of the northern counties. This makes a certain amount of sense since northern Minnesota is not as populated. So, we can see that general population pattern on this map.
